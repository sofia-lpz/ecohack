The Fertilizer API for ECOHACK 2024

Fertex is an interface that provides data about fertilizers and their green house gas emissions.

For use in application development or buisness intelligence

Data gathered from

IPCC - International Panel on Climate Change

UNFCCC - United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change



About IPCC UNFCCC





Why is it important?

Fertex was created to provide knowdlege on the effects on climate change of fertilizers throught the world and throught time in an intuitive and simple way. It allows people and businesses to easily understand the impact of their activities and what thismeans for climate change and the deterioration of the planet’s climate. It was made as a tool for society’s effort to mitigate or reduce the rate at which climate change is taking place.

Information used

Fertex uses data gathered using the standards of IPCC by countries around the world and reported to the UNFCC through the National Inventory Submissions (currently working with the 2023 reports, reflecting information through 1990 to 2021 and only Annex I countries).

The IPCC standards followed are the 2019 Refinement to the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas inventories, which states in it’s fourth volume and eleventh chapter the necessary data to calculate greenhouse gas emissions from fertilizer use.
